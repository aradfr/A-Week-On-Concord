Concord River
Point 1

In 1840, Thoreau began chronicling his 1839 journey with his brother along the Concord and Merrimack Rivers. After his brother's death in 1842, he considered turning the journal entries into a book, ultimately drafting "A Week on the Concord and Merrimack Rivers" at Walden Pond in 1842 and completing it in 1849.

The brothers' journey begins in the chapter titled "Saturday" from Concord, serving as a departure and arrival point for physical and spiritual aspects. On the first day, they travel to Billerica , Massachusetts, and Thoreau intricately weaves their observations into the grand tapestry of universal history.
